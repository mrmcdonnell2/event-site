A day of engaging art and fun for free!

11:00 AM - 5:00PM

September 25th, 2022 — Germantown Avenue in Chestnut Hill, Philadelphia

About Us

Enjoy a day of art, music, culinary delights, and boutique shopping as Germantown Avenue, between Willow Grove and Rex Avenue, is transformed into an outdoor arts and crafts marketplace. Chestnut Hill is a backdrop for more than 100 talented artists throughout the Mid-Atlantic region. A fabulous array of artistic choices is on display, including oils, watercolors, etchings, photography, sculpture, pottery, wood crafts, jewelry, and accessories!

Germantown Avenue will be closed to all vehicular traffic as fine arts and crafts, live entertainment, exhibits, and children’s rides and activities take place up and down the street between the 8000 and 8600 blocks of Germantown Avenue. Visitors can stroll the Avenue to view and purchase fine arts and crafts, enjoy live music or dine al fresco.

Admission to the festival is free!

Vendors

Across the entire festival, various vendors will be stationed, selling arts and goods of a large variety. From food to jewelry, paintings and sculptures, and other dazzling forms of art, there's sure to be something for everyone who visits!

There will be over a hundred vendors to shop from, so have some fun discovering them all!

Sustainability Hub

A new specialty area that began at our Home & Garden Festival will again be focused on green goodness — featuring individuals, makers, and organizations who are working to support the goals of a more sustainable Philadelphia. Speakers will offer educational lectures about what’s happening at the forefront of green innovation in the city, and vendors will share their ethically made local goods.

Funk Fest at Jenks Elementary School

Beanie Bounce Party Rentals will be providing an obstacle course, double slide, basketball game, and multi-sport inflatable games. DJ Tragic will be providing music and announcements. Along with him, SNAPCo will give an entertaining dance performance.

AR Workshop will be offering a $5 make-n-take craft Walls for Justice will be showcasing images of the future Jenks mural and running an activity. FarmerJawn will be conducting a seed planting workshop for kids.

Food strucks will also be found here, including Deke’s BBQ, Burrito Feliz, Lokal French Toast Bites, and Brood Coffee.

Food and Dining

Bring an appetite with you, because there are over a dozen participating restaurants in our events! From bread, ice cream, grilled meat, yogurts, pizza, and drinks, there should be options that everyone can love! Plus, a food market will be offering additional choices. You can eat inside, outside, plus take-out options.

Our local restaurants will be complemented by food trucks at key spots throughout the festival and in the Jenks Academy playground that will be tailored toward food for young children and families.
